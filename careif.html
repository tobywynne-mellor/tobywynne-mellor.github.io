  1. Note taking on technical concepts

Presentation Skills

In this section I learnt what makes a good presentation, how to consider your audience and knowing your onjective. Also we learnt how to find accurate sources by being:

  1. Selective as to not quote irrelevant information
  2. Systematic ensuring that you read sources thoroughly
  3. being electronic so that you make use of all the resources available to us on the internet especially online libraries that QMUL students have exclusive access to
  4. obsessive by checking the accuracy of your information through reading multiple sources.

I learnt lots about how to do a presentation through the task to find the best suited open-source licence for a start-up daily vlogging business called 'vlogga'. As part of wthis I had to design a slide deck presentation which was informative yet easily understandable, as if I was pitching to non-coders but still undergraduates.

Technical Writing

In this part of the module I learnt how to keep my answwers clear and concise, how to utilise feedback and what makes a piece of writing better than another. We were set the task to provide a portfolio of short concept writing that we had already submitted to an other module. We then had to write a revised version and write commentaries - explaining the changes made in the revised versions and the rationale behind it.

Not only will these skills be transferable to my final year project, they have already helped me with the development of my business.

Note Taking

Here the focus was on how to summarise articles, cornell note taking strategy and peer-review. There was an assignment for this part of the module; to write notes about the differences between software licences. To do this we had to do in depth reading of several research papers.

The cornell note taking method has a three section structure.

  • Notes
  • Comments
  • Summary

These all make for a more engaging note taking process. This method allows you to follow the thoughts you had when originally reading from the source. It also makes it easier to do further research on the things you weren't sure about.
